Ethical Issues in DNA Use

Resource 1

URLThe Innocence Project: DNA Exonerations
AuthorThe Innocence Project
AbstractThis page discusses how DNA testing has helped free innocent people who were wrongly convicted. It raises ethical concerns about wrongful imprisonment, the use of untested evidence, and fair trials. The Innocence Project highlights cases where DNA changed lives and influenced reforms. It also advocates for better policies to ensure fairness and prevent mistakes.

Resource 2

URLInside the Cell: The Dark Side of Forensic DNA
AuthorErin Murphy
AbstractThis book explores the hidden problems in forensic DNA use. It explains how misuse, contamination, and overreliance on DNA can lead to injustice. Murphy also discusses issues like racial profiling, flawed lab work, and bias in the courtroom. The book is a warning against treating DNA as infallible, stressing the need for transparency and better standards.
